# EKAM — Brand Guidelines
### Revised Edition · Grounded in Sanskrit, Landscape, and Consistent Luxury
**Version 2.0 · 2026**

## 01 · BRAND ESSENCE

**The Core Truth**

EKAM means "the one" — not because every cabin is different, but because every *stay* belongs singularly to you. The cabin itself is a consistent vessel of comfort and design. What is singular is the *landscape* — each site is chosen because nature has already made it distinctive. We do not design the differentiation. Nature does. We design the consistency of experience *within* that wild differentiation.

**Reframed Pillars:**

### Singular
Not the cabin. The moment. Your arrival at a place no two people will experience the same way, because no two sunsets hit the same ridge the same way. EKAM cabins are identical in craft and comfort — this consistency is the gift. The singularity comes from the land speaking to you, alone.

### Serene
The cabin is designed for quietness. Stone floors. No Wi-Fi by default. A wood stove. A window. The experience is the same at every EKAM — but the view through that window changes everything. Serenity is not about rare architecture; it is about honest design that gets out of the way of what matters.

### Rooted
Every EKAM sits on land that belonged to the forest first. We do not tame it. We build *with* it — respecting the contours, the water flow, the existing growth. This is not wilderness tourism. This is hospitality that knows its place.

## 02 · SANSKRIT INTEGRATION & NOMENCLATURE

**Why Sanskrit matters here:** Sanskrit is not decoration. It is the language of India's philosophical tradition around *being* rather than *having*. EKAM's entire architecture — its naming, its values, its visual language — should breathe Sanskrit meaning without feeling forced or exoticised.

### Master Brand: एकम् (EKAM)
**Meaning:** The One. Unity. Singular presence. Used as the parent brand wordmark.

### Sub-brands (Product tier names — NOT cabin model names):

**कुटीर (KUTIR)**
Meaning: The hut. A simple dwelling in the forest. Humble, sufficient, honest.
- Tier: Standard cabin — 2 adults, shared experiences, essential comfort
- Philosophy: Enough is everything

**वन (VAN)**
Meaning: The forest itself. Dense, alive, breathing.
- Tier: Premium cabin with deeper forest immersion (tents, elevated platforms)
- Philosophy: Live *inside* the wild, safely

**शिखर (SHIKHAR)**
Meaning: The peak. The summit. The highest point of arrival.
- Tier: Luxury retreat — ridgeline cabins, private vistas, full solitude
- Philosophy: You have climbed here. Now breathe.

**Note:** These sub-brand names apply to *the experience tier* — not individual cabin locations. All Kutir cabins worldwide share the same design language and comfort standard. The landscape changes. The cabin integrity does not.

The EKAM wordmark exists in three locked configurations:


### The Bindu as Sacred Anchor

The clay-orange bindu (dot) between E and K in the master wordmark is **not decorative**. It references:
- **Anusvara** (the nasal mark anusvāra in Devanagari script)
- **The point of stillness** before sound
- **The centre** — all movement radiates from this dot

**Rules for bindu deployment:**
- Never below 6px on screen, 2mm in print
- Always in `#B4613A` — never another colour
- Never duplicated or scattered
- Appears only on master wordmark and sub-brand icons (one dot per icon)
- In design layouts: can anchor a header line or act as a single visual punctuation

**Example use:** A horizontal rule ending with a bindu dot. A button with a small bindu above the CTA text. A section break marked only by a centred bindu, breathing space around it.

## 04 · COLOUR PALETTE

**Philosophy:** Earth before accent. The palette comes from an Indian forest at dusk.

| Colour | Hex | Role | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Forest Ink** | `#14201A` | Primary text, deep backgrounds | The darkest part of the forest at night |
| **Deep Forest** | `#2B4630` | Hero backgrounds, primary surface | Forest canopy at dusk |
| **Moss** | `#7A8A6B` | Secondary text, muted labels | Living lichen on bark |
| **Sand** | `#F4EDE1` | Light cards, text panels | Ground at first light |
| **Cream** | `#FAF7F0` | Page background | Morning mist |
| **Bindu Clay** | `#B4613A` | Accent only — buttons, labels, dots | Fired terracotta, sacred objects |
| **Bone** | `#ECE4D3` | Tertiary surface, subtle borders | Weathered wood |
| **Mist** | `#D8D3C4` | Dividers, subtle backgrounds | Fog sitting low |

**Usage ratio we recommend:** 60% Cream/Sand · 30% Forest · 8% Ink · 2% Bindu Clay.

The clay should behave like **saffron in food** — small quantity, unmissable presence. Never flood a layout with clay. It stops being a signature and becomes a billboard.

## 05 · TYPOGRAPHY

### Type System (Three Voices, One Language)

**A. Primary Display — Cormorant Garamond (Light, Regular)**
- **Use for:** Master logo, hero headlines, chapter openers, section titles
- **Weights:** Light (300) for display, Regular (400) for emphasis
- **Letter-spacing:** Always generous — 4px minimum on headlines, 8px+ on wordmarks
- **Style:** Always upright — never italic, never condensed
- **Sizing:** 48px (hero) → 32px (H2) → 22px (H3)
- **Example:** "One cabin. One wild. One you." (light, 48px, 6px letter-spacing)

**B. Secondary Serif — Fraunces (300/400 Italic, 400 Upright)**
- **Use for:** Taglines, pull-quotes, subheadings, email intros, itinerary copy, testimonials
- **Weight:** 300 italic as default voice — 400 upright only for longer body paragraphs
- **Sizing:** 22px (taglines) → 18px (subheads) → 16px (body)
- **Tone:** Warm, slightly literary, never corporate
- **Example:** "Your cabin is waiting. The wood stove is lit. The forest is listening." (italic, 22px, forest green)

**C. Supporting Sans — Inter (300 / 400 / 500)**
- **Use for:** UI labels, buttons, captions, booking forms, metadata, navigation
- **Weight:** 300 for body text, 400 for labels, 500 for small emphasis (buttons, eyebrows)
- **Letter-spacing:** 2–3px on eyebrows, 0.5px on body
- **Sizing:** 14px (body) → 12px (labels) → 11px (eyebrows)
- **Tone:** Clean, efficient, never decorative
- **Example:** "RESERVE YOUR STAY" (500, 11px, letter-spacing 3px)

**D. Devanagari — Tiro Devanagari Hindi (Regular, Italic)**
- **Use for:** Sanskrit brand names, cabin doors (bilingual welcome), wax seals, occasional signage
- **Never:** For long-form English substitution
- **Example:** एकम् · कुटीर · वन · शिखर (on welcome cards, cabin markers, section dividers)
- **Sizing:** 40px (display) → 20px (labels) → 14px (metadata)

## 06 · VOICE & TONE

### How EKAM Speaks

**Register:** A close friend who owns a cabin in the forest and is handing you the key. Warm, unhurried, quietly confident in silence. Hospitality, not hype.

**Sentence structure:** Short. Incomplete. Allowed to breathe.
- ✓ "Your cabin is waiting. The fire is lit."
- ✗ "Your luxurious cabin experience is awaiting your arrival with a fully prepared fireplace."

**Vocabulary:**
- ✓ Forest, stillness, arrival, solitude, wild, unhurried, breathe, fire, tea, mist, dawn, ridge
- ✗ Luxury, deal, offer, book now, unlock, limited time, epic, insane, best-ever, transformation

**Exclamation marks:** None. The weight of the sentence carries the feeling.

**Punctuation:** Period, comma, em-dash allowed. Semicolons used sparingly. Never multiple punctuation.

**First/second person:** You, your, we, our. Never "guests," never third-person distance.

### Core Message Frames (Do / Don't)

| Situation | Do Say | Don't Say |
|---|---|---|
| **What happens at EKAM** | "No Wi-Fi. No television. A kettle, a window, and the mountain." | "Fully equipped smart cabin with 5G, Alexa, and Netflix for your binge weekend." |
| **Booking urgency** | "Your cabin is waiting." | "Book now and save 20% — limited spots left!" |
| **The experience** | "Come as you are. Leave a little lighter." | "Transform your life in 48 hours with our exclusive wellness package." |
| **Comfort level** | "A soft bed at the end of the day. Nothing more needed." | "Luxury amenities meet off-grid authenticity." |
| **What's different** | "Each setting is singular because nature made it that way." | "No two cabins are alike — experience ultimate uniqueness!" |

## 07 · PHOTOGRAPHY & VISUAL CONTENT

### Image Philosophy

Photography is documentation, not storytelling. We show what is there. We do not style it.

**What we shoot:**
- **Interiors:** Natural light only. Empty frames. A chair by a window. A kettle on a stove. The door half-open, forest visible beyond.
- **Exteriors:** Dusk. Pre-dawn. Fog at ground level. The cabin as a small structure in a large landscape — never heroic.
- **Details:** Stone texture. Wood grain. A cup of tea, steam rising. Embers in a fire. The corner of a blanket. Textures of *arrival*, not possession.
- **Landscape:** Wide shots showing the site's natural distinction. No people. No performance. The land speaking for itself.

**What we avoid:**
- Flash photography
- Influencer-style portraits or posed moments
- Colour saturation or heavy filters
- Lifestyle staging (picnics, photogenic setups)
- Drone shots (we stay grounded)
- Hypersmoothed video or fast cuts
- People in the frame (exception: hands holding a cup, silhouettes at dusk)

**Colour treatment:**
- Slight desaturation allowed — boosting earth tones, muting sky
- Never heavily filtered
- Shadows preserved, not crushed
- Whites and creams only slightly warmed
- Never cool blue tones (avoid the "luxury resort" feel)

## 08 · APPLICATION RULES (DO & DON'T)

### Logo & Bindu

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Let the bindu breathe. Never below 6px on screen. | Replace the bindu with a flower, logo mark, or stylised dot. |
| Use wide letter-spacing on EKAM. The calm comes from the space. | Set the wordmark condensed, italic, or in all-lowercase. |
| Deploy the bindu once per section as visual punctuation. | Scatter bindu dots around the layout for "decorative" effect. |
| Use clay accent on buttons, labels, and single focal elements. | Flood layouts with clay. It stops being a signature and becomes a billboard. |

### Typography

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Use Cormorant for headlines with 6px+ letter-spacing. | Use Cormorant for body text. It fatigues the eye at small sizes. |
| Use Fraunces italic for voice-driven copy (taglines, testimonials). | Use Fraunces upright for short captions — reserve upright for longer blocks. |
| Set Inter at 300–500 weight. Keep it restrained. | Mix too many weights or sizes in a single section. |
| Use Devanagari on welcome cards, signage, section dividers. | Substitute Devanagari for English in navigation or long copy. |

### Colour & Contrast

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Keep text on dark forest backgrounds at max contrast (`#F4EDE1` or `#FAF7F0`). | Use white on a light sand background — use forest ink instead. |
| Use clay sparingly — one accent per composition. | Use clay for backgrounds, borders, or secondary elements. |
| Fade clay to 60% opacity if layering over photography. | Overlay clay at full opacity over complex images. |

### Photography & Imagery

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Shoot with natural light, empty frames, quiet moments. | Use flash, influencer-style portraits, or colour-saturated filters. |
| Show the land as its own character — wide, unpeopled, distinctive. | Stage lifestyle moments or use hypersmoothed video. |
| Preserve shadows and textures in post-processing. | Crush shadows or overly brighten underexposed scenes. |

### Voice & Copy

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Write like you're whispering on a porch at dawn. | Use exclamation marks, emojis, or promotional adjectives. |
| Keep sentences short. Let them breathe. | Write run-on clauses or SEO-stuffed paragraphs. |
| Use second person: you, your, we, our. | Distance the reader with "guests," "visitors," or third-person language. |
| Lead with the feeling, then explain the detail. | Lead with features, amenities, or selling points. |

## 11 · RESTRICTED APPLICATIONS (What Not To Do)

- **No EKAM emoji or stylised "E" icon** — the wordmark is complete and sacred
- **No animated logo** — the bindu does not pulse, the wordmark does not shift
- **No social media hashtag variants** — #EkamCabins only, never #BeOneWithNature or #EkamExperience
- **No seasonal colour variations** — the palette remains consistent year-round
- **No illustrated mascots or characters** — EKAM has no personality outside the landscape
- **No corporate gradient overlays** — transparency only, no colour blends
- **No Devanagari in English navigation** — Sanskrit is for poetic/signage moments, not UI

## 12 · PARTNER & COLLABORATION RULES

When collaborating with other brands (tea estates, local artisans, travel partners):

- **Lead with landscape identity** — the location comes first, EKAM partnership is subtle
- **Bindu as co-signature** — small clay dot marking collaborative elements
- **Keep voice consistent** — partners must write in EKAM's calm, unhurried tone
- **Never over-brand** — partner logos appear 50% smaller than EKAM wordmark
- **Use case:** "Tea from Coorg estate, served at EKAM Kutir" — not "EKAM partners with [brand]"
