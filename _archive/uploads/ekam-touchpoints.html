The Touchpoints

Twelve physical artifacts a guest touches, from the road to the wax seal on the morning of departure.
Read paired with the BOS Materials per the Sustainability Roadmap
Preface

What this is.

The Brand Operating System describes the brand in words. This document is the gallery of the artifacts those words point at. Twelve objects, rendered as a designer would draft them before they go to the carpenter, the printer, the potter, the smith.

Each artifact carries the same anatomy: the object, the role it plays in the guest's day, the substrate it is made from (categorical — sources per the Sustainability Roadmap), the typography, the dimensions, and the one line from the doctrine that earned the artifact its place.

The renderings here are designer studies, not photographs. Treat them as briefs for the artisans who will build the final objects in stone, wood, iron, paper, ceramic, and wax.

Touchpoint 01

The Welcome Card

A handwritten card lies on the bed — not the table. Two sentences. Signed by the host with first name only.

Where
On the bed, never on the table. The first thing the guest's hand meets when they sit.
Substrate
Cream Mohawk Superfine 300 gsm, 100% post-consumer fibre. Letterpress masthead. Hand-written body.
Hand
Re-written for each guest from a brief template — never photocopied. The host's own hand.
Signature
First name only. No role title. No "team".

"The cabin smells like it has been aired, not perfumed. The host has been here recently, and is gone."

Touchpoint 02

The Wax Seal

A hand-pressed wax seal in Bindu Clay, stamped with एकम्. The brand's signature, made by hand, in the dawn before departure.

Material
Sealing wax in Bindu Clay #B4613A. Applied warm; cooled in place. Slight irregularity is the artifact's signature.
Stamp
Brass stamp, hand-cut with the एकम् glyph in Tiro Devanagari Hindi. The same stamp across every property in the network.
Dimensions
28–32 mm diameter. Slight oval; never circular by mechanical precision.
Where it appears
Departure morning porch note. Retail twine closures. Pre-stay letterpress envelopes. Never as a decorative graphic.

"The wax seal closes the arc. Inside it: a hand-drawn map of the route home and the seasonal greeting. No signature."

Touchpoint 03

The Match Box

Cream card outside. Sulfur-yellow inside. Strike strip rough on purpose. Functional first; the brand sits at the corner.

Front
Cream cardstock 350 gsm. A single black Bindu at optical centre. The wordmark sits below, half-weight Cormorant Garamond.
Interior
Sulfur-yellow paper. The seasonal property name in Inter 500 along one short edge, in muted ink.
Strike
Phosphorus strip on the lower edge of the sleeve. Deliberately rough to the hand. The match strikes audibly.
Where
Beside every candle in the cabin. The guest lights the cabin themselves on arrival.

"Candles unlit at arrival. Matches placed beside them. The guest lights the cabin themselves."

Touchpoint 04

The Mug

Hand-thrown ceramic in moss. The kettle pours into this — once on arrival, once at the dawn flask, once at every fire-time.

Form
Hand-thrown ceramic. Slightly irregular by hand. No two batches identical. Throwing rings preserved on the surface.
Glaze
Moss-tone matte. Or sand-tone matte. Two batches across the network — never glossy, never coloured beyond the palette.
Bindu
A single Bindu Clay dot at the base, near the foot. Visible when the mug is lifted to drink. Never on the body.
Volume
~320 ml. Enough for the seasonal kettle; not enough to encourage refilling without intention.
Source
Local artisan kiln, named on the in-cabin store hangtag. Per the Sustainability Roadmap artisan register.

"Two plates, two bowls, two mugs, two glasses. Not four. Not six. Two."

Touchpoint 05

The Property Map

Hand-drawn on cream paper, in Forest Ink ink, by the host. Offered at reception on request — not pressed into the guest's hand on arrival.

Substrate
Cream Mohawk Superfine 120 gsm, half-folded. Pocket-sized when folded; opens to one large surface.
Hand
Drawn fresh for each guest by the host. Same paths every time, but the line is the host's. Forest Ink with a fine nib pen.
Markers
The cabin is the Bindu — a single clay dot. The Chai pavilion, the parking terminus, and the trail head are sketched in line. Nothing else.
Footer
The rest is the land. Find your own way. Hand-written in italic, every time.

"No maps in the cabin. The guest is walked through the property on arrival by a staff member."

Touchpoint 06

The Pre-Stay Card

A letterpress card arrives by post a week before the stay. One headline. One small woodcut. A weather note. The host's number, by name.

Press
Letterpress only. No digital printing on guest-facing collateral. The impression is felt; the ink sits below the surface of the paper.
Paper
Cream Mohawk Superfine Eggshell 300 gsm. 100% post-consumer fibre.
Ink
Two colours only. Forest Ink for type. Bindu Clay for the single hairline. Never both at full strength.
Format
A6, single-fold. One paragraph. One image. One phone number. Sealed in an envelope closed with a Bindu Clay wax dot.

"Five guest-facing messages per stay, no more. Confirmation, directions PDF, day-before SMS, post-stay letter, four-week postcard."

Touchpoint 07

The Journal

A bound book left on the porch table on the guest's final night. Photographs and writing from the season's stays. The guest reads it. The guest does not take it.

Binding
Cloth-bound in Deep Forest fabric. Hand-stitched signatures. 32–48 pages per volume.
Cover
एकम् debossed at top. EKAM wordmark debossed centre. Bindu Clay hairline. "A journal of the season" in Fraunces italic.
Contents
Photographs from the season (no guest faces), short writing from the host, an essay from a guest writer when commissioned. Letterpress on Cream Mohawk.
Edition
One volume per season. Numbered. Held in the cabin's porch shelf. Never sold; never given.
Ribbon
Bindu Clay silk ribbon as bookmark. The only printed mark of clay on the artifact.

"A single bound book is left on the porch table — a small collection of photographs and writing from the season's stays. The guest reads it; the guest does not take it."

Touchpoint 08

The Cabin Number Plate

Devanagari numerals — ०३ — hand-cut into local stone, set into the door substrate. Latin numerals appear in operational documents. Never on the cabin itself.

Numerals
Devanagari only. Tiro Devanagari Hindi as the reference. Hand-cut by a mason using local stone.
Substrate
Local stone slab. Source per the Sustainability Roadmap site spec.
Dimensions
≤40 mm numeral height. Plate sized to the numeral with 30 mm margin on each side.
Placement
Beside the door at hand height. Set into the door substrate with four iron pins, mortared with lime, never resin.
Night
Unlit. The cabin is found by the path, not by a numeral visible at 3 am.

"Cabin numbers are in Devanagari at the door. Cast in oxidised iron or hand-cut into local stone. Latin numerals appear in operational documents and booking confirmations; never on the cabin itself."

Touchpoint 09

The Room Identity Plate

Inside the cabin, beside the door at hand height. The only place inside the cabin where the brand wordmark appears in physical form.

Substrate
Indigenous hardwood, hand-finished. Iron inlay for the lettering.
Dimensions
120 mm × 60 mm. Plate thickness 12–15 mm.
Lettering
The full three-word lockup — Ekam · Tier · Location — in Cormorant Garamond, CNC-routed and iron-inlaid. Devanagari beneath, smaller, for the typology and number.
Mounting
Four iron pins, hand-set. No modern screws.
Placement
Interior wall, beside the cabin door, at hand height (1.05–1.15 m from floor). Read on entry, never seen from outside.

"The only place inside the cabin where the brand wordmark appears in physical form. The cabin is not a brand-marked space."

Touchpoint 10

The Arrival Monolith

The first thing the guest meets. A single standing object at the property line. Lit from below at 1800K. Never spot-lit from above.

Substrate
A single piece of local stone, dressed but not polished. Or indigenous hardwood column set into a stone base.
Dimensions
1200–1600 mm tall × 320–400 mm wide × 220–280 mm deep. Taller on exposed approaches.
Lettering
EKAM · TIER · LOCATION — CNC-routed and oxidised-iron-inlaid. Cormorant Garamond Light 300. Cap-height 90 mm.
Lighting
A single floor-set lantern at 1800K, set into the ground to wash the stone from below. Never backlit. Never from above.
Approval
Tier 3 — Founder + Brand Director. Every site is signed off individually.

"The vehicle stops at the stone behind it. The guest steps out and meets the Monolith. From here, the approach is on foot."

Touchpoint 11

The Tiffin

Every hot meal arrives in a three-tier stainless tiffin, wrapped in a cotton napkin, tied with jute twine. The tiffin returns to the kitchen.

Vessel
Three-tier stainless steel. Hand-tinned where the regional artisan tradition supports it. Returnable — never travels home with the guest.
Wrap
Natural undyed cotton napkin, hand-stitched. Folded around the bottom tier, knotted at the top. Replaced when worn, not on schedule.
Tag
Hand-written paper tag tied by twine. Cream paper, Bindu mark, hand-written meal name in the host's hand. Chai sub-brand mark in the corner.
Delivery
Dropped at the porch at the guest's requested hour. The steward does not enter the cabin unless invited.

"All hot meals delivered in three-tier stainless tiffins, wrapped in cotton napkins. The tiffin returns to the kitchen — it does not travel home with the guest."

Touchpoint 12

The Path Lantern

Hand-blown glass globe in an iron cage, on a hardwood post. The lantern marks the primary route. The cabin approach is unlit by design.

Post
Indigenous hardwood, square section. Set in gravel-packed footing — never concrete. 600–900 mm tall.
Cage
Oxidised black iron. Hand-forged. Cap and bottom-cap with three vertical bars holding the globe.
Globe
Hand-blown glass with slight irregularity. Modern flat glass catches headlights; irregular hand-blown glass diffuses.
Lamp
1800K source. Low intensity. CRI 95 minimum. Dusk-to-dawn auto. Maximum 0.4 lux at 5 m.
Placement
Primary route only. 8 m spacing. The cabin approach is unlit by design — the guest carries a hand lantern from reception.

"The walk to the cabin in lantern-light is the start of the arrival."
