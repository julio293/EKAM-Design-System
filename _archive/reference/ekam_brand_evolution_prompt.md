# PROMPT: EVOLVE EKAM BRAND GUIDELINES
## Refined Positioning: "From Seeing to Being There"

---

## **BRIEF TO YOUR DESIGNER/BRAND TEAM**

We're refining EKAM's positioning based on deeper understanding of what guests actually want. This is an evolution, not a rebrand. The core remains; the language shifts from exclusive/rare to welcoming/real.

### **Current Positioning Issue:**
Our previous language around "view living" and "inhabitation" felt exclusive—like you had to be a certain type of guest or earn the right to be there. That's not who we are.

### **The Refined Positioning:**
**From:** "We sell temporal ownership of rare views"
**To:** "We bring views to life for the people who genuinely want to be there"

**From:** "Live inside landscapes you've only seen from outside"
**To:** "Experience places the way they actually feel, not how they look online"

This is softer, more inviting, still true to the core experience.

---

## **CORE INSIGHT (Reframed)**

**What guests are actually seeking:**
They've scrolled past beautiful mountain photos for years. They've saved Instagram posts. They've wanted to know what those places *really feel like* beyond the filter.

**What we offer:**
A chance to finally experience the place as it actually is—the specific texture, light, and feeling of that exact location. Not a version of it. The real thing.

**Why this matters:**
When you finally arrive and sit on the porch of a cabin in Chitkul, the view isn't aspirational anymore. It becomes real. That shift—from aspiration to reality—is the entire experience.

## **REVISED PROPERTY DESCRIPTIONS (Welcoming, Not Gatekeeping)**

### **EKAM SHIKHAR — The High-Altitude Experience**
Not: "Live at the altitude you've never actually seen"
**Yes:** "The mountains feel different at this altitude. Come experience that difference."

- Located at 3200m+ in Kalpa, Nako
- Higher elevation means thinner air, clearer light, fewer people
- You'll notice how the weather moves across valleys. How sunlight behaves differently. How quiet deepens.
- Not exclusive. Just real.

### **EKAM KUTIR — The Valley Experience**
Not: "Live in the view you've photographed"
**Yes:** "These valleys are beautiful for a reason. Now find out what that reason feels like."

- Located at 2100-2400m in Chitkul, Sangla
- You pass through these valleys on the drive. Most people keep driving.
- We invite you to stop. To sit in the place instead of moving through it.
- The view isn't better from inside the cabin. But it's *different*—you're part of the landscape, not admiring it from a distance.

### **EKAM VAN — The Forest Experience**
Not: "Live inside dense forest texture"
**Yes:** "Forests are impossible to photograph. But they're incredible to actually be in."

- Located in Baspa Valley jungle canopy (1800-2200m)
- You'll understand why jungle photos never capture it—it's not visual. It's the air. The sound. The feeling of being held by density.
- This is what it feels like to actually be inside a forest, not looking at one.

- Honest. Straightforward. Here's the place.

- Better light, better positioning
- Still minimal, still real, still the place—just with more ease
- For guests who want the experience + the ability to settle in more

## **PHOTOGRAPHY DIRECTION (Still Foreground-Focused, But Warmer)**

### **What to Shoot:**
- Guests actually doing what guests do (reading, sitting, having tea)
- The specific light of that place at that time
- The texture of habitation (worn wood, weathered canvas, actual use)
- The actual landscape from inside the cabin (not distance shots)
- A full day arc of the same view (morning through evening)

### **What to Avoid:**
- Aspirational golden-hour perfection
- Empty cabins (it's a place to be in, not look at)
- Instagram reels and short clips
- Curated "moments"
- Professional photoshoots disguised as guest moments

### **The Vibe:**
"This is what it actually feels like to be here"—not "This is how we want you to imagine it."

**Not:**
- High occupancy rate
- Instagram likes
- Strong booking funnel conversion
- "Views" or "impressions"

- Repeat visitors (view-living is addictive)

- Long-form reviews and essays from guests

1. **Audit website copy:** Replace all language around scarcity, exclusivity, rarity, and possession. Shift toward real experience, authentic feeling, actual presence.

2. **Rephrase property pages:** Move from "live in" language to "experience" language. Make it inviting, not gatekeeping.

4. **Revise guest communication:**
   - Pre-arrival: Clarify what you're getting (authentic place, not luxury hotel)
   - At arrival: Welcome them to the place, not the cabin
   - Post-departure: Thank them for being there, not for being your guest

5. **Align team language:** Train staff to guide guests toward genuine experience, not optimized comfort. "You're here to experience this place as it is" not "We've optimized your comfort."

6. **Rethink partnerships:** Focus on editorial (essay-driven) rather than influencer (image-driven).

## **THE DIFFERENCE THIS MAKES**

**From scarcity-based (risky):**
"Only rare people can appreciate this. Are you one of them?"
→ Excludes most guests. Creates wrong expectations. Feels precious.

**To experience-based (inclusive):**
"You've wondered what these places feel like. We have the answer."
→ Includes everyone curious. Sets right expectations. Feels generous.

Both are true. The second is more honest and more welcoming.
