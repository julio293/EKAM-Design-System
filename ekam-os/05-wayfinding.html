Read by daylight. Walked by moonlight.

Three trail systems · six arrow glyphs · cedar trail-marker posts · distance and grade markings · the no-artificial-light-after-dusk rule formalised. Wayfinding moves the guest without instructing them. The guest reads the post once, then walks.

Aमार्ग · the routes

Three trails. One short. One quiet. One full.

Every EKAM property offers exactly three trails. Not more — choice paralysis defeats restraint. The trails are calibrated so any guest can complete the short before lunch and the full before dusk.

कोमल · the gentle

The short walk

1.2 km · ~25 min · gentle

Loop around the cabin cluster and the kitchen garden. Flat. Suitable in slippers if needed. This is the walk a guest takes before breakfast.

4 markers
शांत · the quiet

The forest path

3.2 km · ~75 min · moderate

Into the forest above the property to a single bench at a clearing. One small ascent. Returns the same way. The walk a guest takes after lunch.

8 markers · 1 bench
पूर्ण · the full

The ridge walk

7.4 km · ~3 hr · steep

Up to the ridgeline. View opens halfway. Returns via the lower path past the stream. The walk a guest takes once. Carry water and tell Aarav.

14 markers · 1 lookout post
Bदिशा · direction

Six arrow glyphs. No others.

All wayfinding uses one of six. The vocabulary is closed — turn left, turn right, continue, climb, descend, return. Compound directions (sharp left, hairpin) collapse to the closest of the six.

Continue Forward along the path
Return Back to where you came
Turn left Branch left at the path
Turn right Branch right at the path
Climb Ascend · gradient ahead
Descend Down · careful in wet
Cस्तम्भ · the post

The trail-marker post. Cedar, branded, eye-level.

A single cedar post per marker. Hot-iron-branded with the arrow + cabin/landmark name + distance remaining. No painted lettering — the brand is burnt in. Posts weather to silver-grey over a season.

MaterialLocal deodar cedar · 100 × 100 mm square post · top-chamfered 45° · tung-oiled charcoal-tint
Height1500 mm above ground · 400 mm buried in concrete · 1900 mm overall

Dअंधेरा · the dark

No artificial light after dusk on paths.

A formal rule. After 19:00, the only light on the property is the cabin window. Paths read by moonlight. If a guest needs to walk a trail in the dark, they carry a lantern from reception. The rule is what makes the night sky.

The exception list

Exactly three exceptions exist. Any other request requires founder sign-off and a `_meta/decisions.md` entry.

1 · Cabin doorstep lantern. A single oil lantern at each cabin step. Lit by the host at dusk; extinguished at 22:00. 5 W equivalent · warm clay glow. Burns ~3 hours of kerosene.

2 · Reception path lantern. One lantern at the gate. Lit 19:00–22:00. Hand-lit, not switch-lit.

3 · Emergency · medical or evacuation. Headlamps in a steel box at reception. Used only by staff during an incident. Otherwise sealed.

What we don't do

No solar-LED path stakes. No motion sensors. No bollard lighting. No spotlights on signage. No "downlights for safety" on stairs. The brand is darker than industry-standard hospitality on purpose — the dark is a service, not a constraint.

Fदर्शन · the view

The lookout post. One per trail.

At the high point of the ridge walk, a single cedar bench and a simple brass plate naming what is visible. Mountains, valleys, the river, a village. Named, not numbered.

BenchLocal cedar · 1800 × 400 × 450 mm · tung-oiled charcoal · single plank, no back
View plateBrass · 200 × 80 mm · etched · mounted on pearwood block beside bench
LetteringCormorant 400 · names of peaks visible · italic Cormorant for distance ("Kinnaur Kailash · 24 km")
Bindu3 mm clay separator between English and Devanagari names of the same peak
RefusalsNo "scenic point" plaque · no QR code · no telephoto binocular stand · no fee box
