— 03 · Materials द्रव्य · the material

Eight materials. One palette, every property.

The physical brand is built from eight materials and nothing else. Every cabin, every plate, every plaque, every artifact on site uses this palette. New materials require founder + head-of-brand sign-off. The discipline of the palette is what makes the second property recognisable as EKAM without seeing a wordmark.

Aपट्ट · the palette

Eight materials. Each with a role.

Three primary surfaces, three accent surfaces, two artifact materials. Vendor-ready specs: dimensions, finish, weather rating, longevity, replacement cycle.

01

Forest plate · the default outdoor

The signage workhorse. Cabin plates, gate panels, wayfinding posts.

Substrate2 mm mild steel sheet · laser-cut
FinishPowder-coat RAL 6009 (Forest #2B4630) · matte 10% gloss
LetteringCream vinyl decal · 200 µm cast vinyl · 7-year outdoor
WeatherIP65 · UV-stable · monsoon-tested
Lifespan7–10 years in Himalayan conditions
CycleInspect annually · re-paint at 7 yr · re-vinyl at 5 yr
02

Brass · the heritage plate

Cabin door plate, wax seal stamp, retail tag. Develops patina.

Substrate3 mm sand-cast brass (CuZn37) · sandblasted matte
FinishAcid-etched lettering · raw oiled · no lacquer
MountBrass screws into pearwood backplate
WeatherOutdoor-rated · develops forest-green patina
Lifespan50+ years · patina is feature, not defect
CycleOil once a year (light tung) · never polish bright
03

Pearwood · the warm interior

Stamp handles, journal covers, kitchen utensils, switch plates.

SubstrateIndian pearwood (Pyrus pashia) · kiln-dried 8% MC
FinishHand-sanded · natural beeswax · matte
EtchingLaser-etched marks · burned 0.4 mm deep
WeatherIndoor only · cracks in monsoon if exposed
Lifespan15–25 years with annual wax
CycleRe-wax annually · re-sand at 10 yr · replace if cracked
04

Cream paper plaque · the indoor instruction

Common-area signage, kitchen rules, journal pages, welcome letter.

Substrate240 gsm cream cotton-rag · acid-free
PrintingScreen-print forest ink · 1-colour · oversized for plates
MountBrass push-pin into pearwood frame (no glass)
WeatherIndoor only · humidity-sensitive
Lifespan2–4 years before fade
CycleReplace seasonally if message changes · re-print every 3 yr
05

Cedar timber · the cabin skin

Cabin cladding, trail markers, fire-pit boundary, deck planks.

SubstrateLocal deodar cedar · 18 mm vertical board · ship-lap
FinishTung oil · charcoal-tinted (40% black) · 3 coats
MarksHot iron brand · 8–12 mm cap height · no paint
WeatherOutdoor · weathers to silver-grey over 18 months
Lifespan25–40 years with oil refresh
CycleRe-oil every 3 yr · replace boards as needed
06

Dनिषेध · the refusals

Materials we do not use. Even when they are cheaper.

Each of these has been proposed by a vendor at some point. Each is named here so the decision is not re-litigated when the next vendor proposes it.

— Removed from consideration

  • ✕Acrylic / Perspex / PVCCheap, available, weather-proof — and reads as municipal signage. The brand is a forest property, not a bus stop.
  • ✕Aluminium composite (ACP)Industry-standard for hotel signage. Looks identical to the airport, the mall, the highway. Used precisely nowhere in EKAM.
  • ✕Stainless steelToo reflective, too clinical. Our brass develops patina; steel never does. Use only for hidden hardware.
  • ✕Glass-faced signsPlate inside a glass case. Reads as bus-stand advertising. We never frame a plate; the plate is the frame.
  • ✕Backlit / LED-edge signsNo internal illumination, anywhere. The cabin's lit window is the only thing that glows on site after dusk.
  • ✕Synthetic stone (Corian, quartz)Looks like stone, isn't. Where stone is needed, local sandstone is used — even at higher cost.
  • ✕Brass-coloured paint / brass laminateBrass is brass. Paint reads as paint within six months. If we cannot afford brass for a surface, we use forest plate instead.
  • ✕Plastic twine / nylon stringSynthetic ties on a journal that's meant to compost in 25 years. Jute, always.
Eसम्पूर्ण · the total

Eight materials. Everything else is removed.

A new material requires written sign-off from the founders and the head of brand. The default response is no. The system is the discipline.

The reason the palette is small is not aesthetic — it is operational. With eight materials, every property runs the same maintenance schedule, sources from the same four vendors, and renders the brand identically. With twelve, the second property starts to drift; with twenty, it becomes someone else's property under our wordmark.

The hardest decision in a hospitality system is not what to include. It is what to refuse. The list above is more brand than any artifact in this document.
